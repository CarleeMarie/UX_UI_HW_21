How to Get Started

Bonsai is becoming an ever more popular hobby - especially for those seeking a hobby that calms the mind and is not physically strenuous. Bonsai can become a lifelong hobby - because it takes time and practice to "master" it. Even bonsai masters will tell you that the learning and growth in bonsai never stops. It is important to keep in mind that no one starts out as a master - you will have plants that don't make it or that get unruly. This is normal. The most important thing is to not give up and seek out information and ask other enthusiasts for advice. While bonsai is generally a solitary hobby - once you find other bonsai enthusiasts, you will find that there is quite an active, social community to join if you so choose.

Step 1: Decide

The very first decision you will need to make is if you want to try starting bonsai from seed or buying a plant that you can turn into bonsai (or a young bonsai). Each has their benefits and drawbacks.

Step 3: Directions for Care

Pots: Using the right sized pot for the size of bonsai you want is critical, as pot size determines how big the plant will grow. It is better to start out with something a bit small and move to something bigger later if you so choose. Restricting roots and root space is one of the main ways of controlling growth, so choose carefully.

Water: Watering your bonsai the right amount is critical to keeping you bonsaid healthy and happy. If you live in a dry climate or in a cold place where you use heating, you will need to keep humidity in mind as well. A good misting bottle is a wise investment as it is a good way to keep your bonsai healthy in between waterings.

Light: The light that your bonsai needs will depend on the kind of plant it is. Some plants require much more sunlight than others. When deciding on what kind of bonsai you will have, make note of the sunniest spots in your home and the ability to have plants put near those light sources. In northern states, a grow light is a good idea for the winter months when daylight hours are shorter.
